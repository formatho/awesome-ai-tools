# H4 Partnership Agreement Template - Case Study Collaboration

Created: 2026-04-22
Status: Ready for execution when Formatho access is available

---

## Overview

This template provides a lightweight partnership agreement for case study collaborations between Formatho and partner companies. The agreement is designed to be:

- **Simple:** Single-page, easy to understand
- **Fair:** Balanced benefits for both parties
- **Low-friction:** Minimal legal review required
- **Execution-focused:** Clear roles, timelines, and success metrics

**Note:** This is NOT legal advice. For significant partnerships or sensitive data sharing, consult with legal counsel.

---

## Partnership Agreement - Case Study Collaboration

**Date:** [Date]
**Effective Date:** [Date]

**Parties:**
- **Partner A:** Formatho ([legal entity name]), represented by Premchand, Founder
- **Partner B:** [Partner Company Name], represented by [Name], [Title]

---

### 1. Purpose

The parties agree to collaborate on creating and publishing a joint case study showcasing how Formatho and [Partner Company Name]'s products work together to solve developer problems.

### 2. Scope of Collaboration

**2.1 Case Study Creation**
- Partner A (Formatho) will research, write, and design the case study
- Partner B ([Partner Company Name]) will provide technical input, feedback, and approval
- Both parties will agree on the final content before publication

**2.2 Content Structure**
The case study will follow industry-standard B2B case study format:
- **Challenge (15-20%):** Customer pain point before using our products
- **Solution (40-50%):** How Formatho + [Partner Company Name] solved the problem (including code snippets, architecture diagrams, technical details)
- **Results (30-40%):** Quantitative metrics (latency, uptime, time saved, cost reduction) and qualitative outcomes

**2.3 Distribution Channels**
Both parties agree to distribute the case study through their respective channels:

**Partner A (Formatho) Channels:**
- Formatho blog
- Formatho Twitter/X
- Formatho LinkedIn
- Reddit (relevant subreddits)
- Dev.to / Medium
- Hacker News
- Formatho Discord/community

**Partner B ([Partner Company Name]) Channels:**
- [Partner Company Name] blog
- [Partner Company Name] newsletter
- [Partner Company Name] social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn)
- [Partner Company Name] Discord/community
- Other channels: [specify]

**2.4 Timeline**
- **Week 1:** Concept approval and research
- **Week 2:** First draft and feedback
- **Week 3:** Final revisions and approval
- **Week 4:** Publication and distribution
- **Weeks 5-6:** Amplification and lead tracking

### 3. Roles and Responsibilities

**3.1 Partner A (Formatho) Responsibilities:**
- Research and draft the case study
- Design graphics and diagrams
- Coordinate with Partner B for technical input
- Set up tracking (UTM parameters, analytics)
- Distribute to Formatho channels
- Provide weekly performance reports

**3.2 Partner B ([Partner Company Name]) Responsibilities:**
- Provide technical input and feedback on drafts
- Approve final content before publication
- Distribute to [Partner Company Name] channels
- Provide access to analytics (if available)
- Respond to inbound leads from the case study

**3.3 Joint Responsibilities:**
- Cross-promote on social media (tag each other)
- Both parties approve any major changes to distribution plan
- Both parties provide honest feedback to improve future collaborations

### 4. Benefits to Both Parties

**4.1 Mutual Benefits:**
- **Lead Generation:** Expect 3-5 inbound leads for both parties within 14 days of publication
- **Audience Cross-Pollination:** Access to each other's developer communities
- **Content Creation:** Formatho handles heavy lifting; [Partner Company Name] gets high-quality content
- **Social Proof:** Real-world example of both products in action
- **Brand Awareness:** Exposure across multiple channels (blog, social, communities)

**4.2 Partner B ([Partner Company Name]) Specific Benefits:**
- Zero engineering effort required
- Content created and designed for free
- Distribution to Formatho's growing developer community
- Evergreen asset (case studies drive leads for months/years)
- Trackable attribution (UTM parameters for lead source tracking)

**4.3 Partner A (Formatho) Specific Benefits:**
- Association with established [Partner Company Name] brand
- Access to [Partner Company Name]'s larger developer community
- Validation from working with respected partner
- Content for Formatho marketing pipeline

### 5. Tracking and Attribution

**5.1 Lead Attribution**
- All links in the case study will include UTM parameters for tracking
- Partner A will set up tracking dashboard to monitor traffic and conversions
- Partner B will provide access to their analytics (if available)
- Both parties will share weekly performance reports for the first 2 weeks

**5.2 UTM Structure**
Links will be formatted as:
```
https://partner.com/pricing?utm_source=formatho&utm_medium=case_study&utm_campaign=joint_case_study&utm_content=[partner_name]
```

**5.3 Success Metrics**
The collaboration will be considered successful if:
- Case study receives 500+ combined views across all channels
- Conversion rate is 1-3% (industry benchmark)
- Each party receives 3-5 inbound leads within 14 days
- Case study is shared or discussed on social media (Twitter, LinkedIn, Reddit)

### 6. Intellectual Property and Usage Rights

**6.1 Ownership**
- The case study content is jointly owned by both parties
- Both parties have the right to use, modify, and distribute the content
- Neither party may claim exclusive ownership of the joint work

**6.2 Usage Rights**
- Both parties may use the case study for marketing, sales, and promotional purposes
- Both parties may share, repost, or reference the case study in any channel
- Attribution should be given to both companies when sharing

**6.3 Third-Party Use**
- Third parties (media, blogs, communities) may share the case study with attribution
- Neither party may sell or license the case study to third parties without written consent

**6.4 Trademarks and Logos**
- Both parties grant each other a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use their trademarks and logos in the case study
- Use must be consistent with each party's brand guidelines
- Either party may request removal of their logo/branding at any time

### 7. Confidentiality

**7.1 Information Sharing**
- Both parties may share non-confidential information for the purpose of creating the case study
- Sensitive information (customer data, revenue figures, unreleased features) should not be included without written consent

**7.2 Customer Consent**
- If using a real customer story, both parties must obtain written consent from the customer
- If using a fictional customer, clearly state that the example is illustrative

**7.3 Data Protection**
- Both parties agree to protect any personal data shared during the collaboration
- All data handling will comply with applicable privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

### 8. Term and Termination

**8.1 Term**
This agreement is effective from the Effective Date and continues until:
- The case study is published and distributed (estimated 4 weeks), OR
- Either party terminates the agreement with 7 days written notice

**8.2 Termination**
Either party may terminate this agreement at any time with 7 days written notice if:
- The other party breaches any material term of this agreement
- The collaboration is not progressing as expected
- Either party decides the partnership is no longer aligned with their goals

**8.3 Termination Effects**
Upon termination:
- Both parties retain the right to use already-created content
- Both parties should remove or update live content if requested by the other party
- Neither party will be liable for any losses or damages resulting from termination

### 9. No Financial Obligations

**9.1 No Payment**
This is a non-monetary collaboration. Neither party will pay the other for this partnership.

**9.2 Revenue Sharing**
If the case study generates revenue (e.g., premium content, gated downloads), revenue will be shared 50/50 between both parties unless otherwise agreed in writing.

**9.3 Future Opportunities**
If this collaboration is successful, both parties may explore deeper partnerships (revenue sharing, joint features, co-marketing) in the future. Such future partnerships would require a separate agreement.

### 10. Representations and Warranties

**10.1 Accuracy**
Both parties represent that all information they provide for the case study is accurate to the best of their knowledge.

**10.2 Authority**
Both parties represent that they have the authority to enter into this agreement and approve content for their respective companies.

**10.3 Compliance**
Both parties agree to comply with all applicable laws and regulations in creating and distributing the case study.

### 11. Limitation of Liability

**11.1 No Warranties**
This case study is provided "as is." Neither party makes any warranties, express or implied, regarding the accuracy, completeness, or usefulness of the content.

**11.2 Limitation of Damages**
In no event shall either party be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages arising from this collaboration.

### 12. Dispute Resolution

**12.1 Good Faith**
Both parties agree to work in good faith to resolve any disputes that may arise.

**12.2 Mediation**
If disputes cannot be resolved informally, both parties agree to engage in mediation before pursuing legal action.

**12.3 Governing Law**
This agreement shall be governed by the laws of [jurisdiction, e.g., State of California / United Kingdom / etc.].

### 13. General Provisions

**13.1 Entire Agreement**
This agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions, agreements, or understandings.

**13.2 Amendments**
Any amendments to this agreement must be in writing and signed by both parties.

**13.3 Severability**
If any provision of this agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**13.4 Assignment**
Neither party may assign this agreement without written consent from the other party.

**13.5 Waiver**
Failure of either party to enforce any provision of this agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that provision.

## Checklist: Before Signing Agreement

**For Formatho:**
- [ ] Partner company background check (website, reviews, community reputation)
- [ ] Verify contact person's authority (can they approve content?)
- [ ] Confirm distribution channels exist (blog, newsletter, social media)
- [ ] Estimate partner's audience size (developer count, engagement)
- [ ] Check for competitive conflicts (do they work with our competitors?)

**For Partner Company (ask them to verify):**
- [ ] Confirm authority to approve case study content
- [ ] Confirm distribution channels are available
- [ ] Confirm team bandwidth to review and provide feedback
- [ ] Confirm no competitive conflicts with their other partnerships

**Legal Review (if needed):**
- [ ] For significant partnerships (enterprise customers, sensitive data), consult legal counsel
- [ ] For simple collaborations, email-based agreement is sufficient
- [ ] If unsure, start with email agreement and upgrade to formal agreement later

## Notes and Customization

### When to Use Formal Agreement vs. Email Agreement

**Use Formal Agreement (single-page doc) when:**
- Partner is an established enterprise company
- Collaboration involves sensitive customer data
- Partner requests a formal agreement
- Future deeper partnerships are likely

**Use Email Agreement when:**
- Partner is a startup or small company
- Collaboration is simple and low-risk
- Partner prefers lightweight approach
- Speed is important (email agreement can be executed in hours)

### Customization Options

**Add these sections if relevant:**

**Exclusivity Clause:**
"Both parties agree not to create similar case studies with direct competitors for 90 days after publication."

**Revenue Sharing:**
"If the case study is used as a gated download or paid content, revenue will be shared 50/50 between parties."

**Performance Bonus:**
"If the case study exceeds 10 inbound leads in 14 days, both parties agree to explore a deeper partnership (e.g., referral program, co-marketing campaign)."

**Long-Term Partnership:**
"If this collaboration is successful, both parties agree to discuss long-term partnership opportunities (marketplace integration, joint features, revenue sharing)."

### Red Flags to Watch For

**Partner Requests:**
- Exclusivity that limits future partnerships (be careful!)
- Revenue share on case study content (unusual for marketing collab)
- Ownership of Formatho content (should be joint ownership)
- Approval rights over all Formatho content (should be limited to case study only)
- Upfront payment or fees (this should be non-monetary)

**If Partner Asks for Changes:**
- Review carefully against Formatho's interests
- Consult legal counsel if unsure
- Don't agree to anything that limits future partnerships
- Keep the agreement simple and balanced
