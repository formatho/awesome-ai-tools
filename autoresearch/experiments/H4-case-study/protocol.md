# H4 Protocol: Case Study Partnership

**Experiment ID:** H4
**Hypothesis:** Case Study Partnership
**Status:** Research Phase
**Date Created:** 2026-04-20
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-20

## Hypothesis Statement

**Name:** Case Study Partnership
**Prediction:** Creating a joint case study will generate ≥3 inbound leads within 14 days

**Mechanistic Rationale:**
Case studies serve as social proof and demonstrate real-world value. When published through partner channels (blogs, newsletters, social media), they reach qualified audiences actively seeking solutions. Companies with complementary products have shared incentives to co-promote success stories.

**Why we expect this to work:**
1. B2B buyers trust peer validation (studies show 92% trust referrals/case studies)
2. Partners already have audiences of qualified developers
3. Joint case studies cross-pollinate audiences
4. No technical integration required - just storytelling
5. Can be executed without product access (use early adopters, pilot users)

## Success Metric

**Primary:** ≥3 inbound leads from case study within 14 days

**Definition of "inbound lead":**
- Email or contact form submission
- Expresses interest in Formatho
- Can be attributed to the case study (UTM tracking, reference URL)
- Qualified (not spam, relevant use case)

**Secondary metrics:**
- Case study views/readership
- Social media engagement
- Partner promotion reach
- Website traffic attribution

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Partner Identification (Days 1-2)
**Criteria:**
1. Complementary product (blockchain infra, dev tools, AI agents)
2. Existing customer overlap
3. Active audience (blogs, newsletters, social media)
4. Willingness to co-promote

**Target partners (initial list):**
- QuickNode customers using AI agents
- Alchemy customers with multi-chain needs
- Tooling platforms (LangChain, AutoGPT, etc.)
- Developer tooling platforms (Postman, Insomnia)
- Infrastructure providers (Cloudflare, AWS Marketplace)

**Approach method:**
- Identify mutual customers via social proof
- Reach out via LinkedIn/email with case study proposal
- Offer reciprocal case study or content

### Phase 2: Case Study Creation (Days 3-7)
**Story structure:**
1. **Challenge:** What problem was the customer solving?
2. **Solution:** How Formatho + Partner solved it
3. **Results:** Quantitative metrics and outcomes
4. **Technical details:** How it works (for technical audience)
5. **CTA:** How others can implement

**Formats:**
- Long-form blog post (partner's blog)
- Short-form case study (website)
- Video testimonial
- Social media thread
- Slide deck (for sales/enterprise)

### Phase 3: Publication & Promotion (Days 8-14)
**Distribution channels:**
1. Partner's blog/newsletter
2. Formatho blog/newsletter
3. LinkedIn/Twitter/X posts
4. Reddit communities (r/ethereumdev, r/solana, etc.)
5. Dev.to, Medium, Hacker News
6. Industry newsletters (Bankless, The Defiant, etc.)

**Tracking:**
- UTM parameters on all links
- Google Analytics campaign tracking
- Partner referral codes
- Lead source attribution

### Phase 4: Lead Capture (Days 8-14+)
**CTA mechanisms:**
- "Learn how to implement this" → demo signup
- "Get started with Formatho" → free tier signup
- "Talk to our team" → contact form
- "Get the code" → email capture

**Lead qualification:**
- Ask: "What's your use case?"
- Ask: "What infrastructure are you using?"
- Ask: "Timeline for implementation?"

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Can't find a partner with overlapping customer
**Mitigation:**
- Cast wide net across multiple ecosystems
- Use public social proof (Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Consider "fictional" case study based on hypothetical use case

### Risk 2: Partner declines or delays
**Mitigation:**
- Outreach to multiple partners in parallel
- Offer reciprocal value (case study, cross-promotion)
- Have backup stories ready

### Risk 3: Case study doesn't resonate with audience
**Mitigation:**
- Test multiple angles (technical, business, ROI)
- A/B test headlines and formats
- Get partner feedback before publication

### Risk 4: Leads are low quality
**Mitigation:**
- Add qualifying questions to CTAs
- Use gated content (case study PDF in exchange for email)
- Target channels with developer audiences
